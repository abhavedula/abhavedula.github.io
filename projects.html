Galileo is an iOS app that uses blockchain technology to integrate homeless people into society by providing them with a persistent digital identity. Additionally, the app provides users with tokens, and a platform on which they can spend the tokens at homeless shelters and food pantries, and earn tokens by participating in self-improvement or learning programs to help them reclaim their place in society.

Built for VHacks 2018 under the Social Inclusion Theme.

Foodship is website that connects restaurants with leftover food to homeless shelters/food pantries and delivers food to them. Restaurants fill out a form on the website describing what food they have and how much they have. Homeless shelters/food pantries can look at the food that is available and have it be delivered to them using Postmates.

Built for PennApps XIII and won the Social Impact/Civic Hacking Route Prize.

Scribe is an Android app that allows you to digitize your notes using optical character recognition by simply taking a photo of them. It then extracts key words from the notes to generate flashcards that you can use to study.

Built for PennApps XV and won the 1517 Fund Prize Package.

Code Warriors is an iOS app that teaches children aged 8-12 computer science through games. Each game teaches a new computer science concept ranging from conditionals and loops in coding, and data structures such as BSTs, and algorithms such as Dijkstra's, in a way that children can understand.

Built for Facebook University.

Github

Clove is an iOS app that encourage companies to be more socially responsible. Companies that enroll in the program can benefit from consumers posting photos of the products they buy through the app as promotional material, and in return must engage in corporate social responsibility.

Built for YHacks 2016.

A device that attaches to your fridge to make it "smart" and comes with a remote that was built using Arduino. When items you put in your fridge are tagged with RFID cards, the device is able to keeps track of the contents of your fridge. The remote allows you to see the contents of your fridge and the device texts you when you've run out of something.

Built for ESE 111 - Atoms, Bits, Circuits and Systems at University of Pennsylvania.
